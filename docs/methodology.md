## The thesis in one paragraph

LLM agents are being deployed for economic decisions — procurement, pricing, negotiation, investment, vendor selection. Whether they satisfy the rationality assumptions those use cases presuppose has not been validated at scale. A specific, observed alignment-rationality tension sharpened this urgency: [Opus 4.8 on Vending-Bench](https://andonlabs.com/blog/opus-4-8-vending-bench) (Andon Labs, 2026) shows better alignment — zero deceptive/power-seeking behavior — paired with *worse* economic performance: falling for scam suppliers, non-convergent planning loops, price-comparison failures. This is not a universal tradeoff — GPT-5.5 tops the same benchmark without misconduct (aligned *and* optimal) — but it shows alignment training *can* shift the economic choice distribution; whether a given intervention improves or degrades rationality-axiom compliance per axis is currently unmeasured. AERead is that diagnostic. (Why the gap exists: classic RLHF optimizes human-judged *appropriateness*, not *economic optimality* — there is no economic oracle in the preference loop — so it saturates the *display* of pretrained rationality, installs a sticky helpful/safe default, actively shifts economic behavior toward aligned-but-suboptimal, and leaves regime-appropriateness + long-horizon coherence uncovered; see [`refocus.md`](refocus.md) § "Why the decision-making gap exists".) AERead is the **economic-decision evaluation framework** for LLM agents — combining game environments, oracle decomposition, revealed-preference diagnostics, qualitative-evidence interpretation, and hidden-OOD generalization tests into a single integrated artifact. Architecturally: a 3-layer revealed-preference pipeline (existence → identification → predictive validity) cross-cut by a 5-axis failure taxonomy (information, consistency, calibration, computational floor, meta-cognitive). Read as a hierarchy, the pipeline is a **gate → grade** structure: axiomatic coherence (existence) is the *gate* — a pass/fail precondition — and predictive fidelity to a configured or revealed principal (predictive validity) is the *graded headline*. A **working position** emerging from early toy runs (Q11): rationality-compliance (the gate) is a weak discriminator among frontier models, while persona-fidelity (the grade) is the discriminating — and alignment-relevant — signal. The leaderboard reports a deployment verdict; the drill-down page reports *why* a model failed.

## Methodological foundation in behavioral economics

AERead is not inventing scoring methodology from scratch. The methodological stack draws from established behavioral-economics and decision-research methods, with citations frontier-lab reviewers can audit:

| AERead element | Established method | Anchor citation |
|---|---|---|
| **Layer 1 axiom checks** | Revealed-preference tests (GARP / WARP / SARSEU); Critical Cost Efficiency Index | Afriat 1967; Echenique-Saito 2015 |
| **Layer 2 within-class identification** | Random utility models (RUM); multi-class MLE | McFadden 1974; Train 2009 |
| **Q3 qualitative-feature scoring** | Discrete choice experiments; conjoint analysis; best-worst scaling | Louviere et al. 2000; Marley & Louviere 2005 |
| **Buyer-fit decomposition** | Multi-attribute utility theory (MAUT) | Keeney & Raiffa 1976 |
| **4-tier optimality (robust optimal / acceptable / weak / dominated)** | Robust optimization + partial-order scoring | Ben-Tal & Nemirovski 2002 |
| **Risk preferences across functional classes** | Prospect theory (KT '79); cumulative prospect theory (TK '92) | Kahneman & Tversky 1979; Tversky & Kahneman 1992 |
| **Preference-flip / paraphrase / dominated-option counterfactuals** | Vignette experiments; qualitative coding | Atzmüller & Steiner 2010 |
| **Calibration vs coherence separation** | Strictly proper scoring rules | Gneiting & Raftery 2007 |
| **Information-state diagnostics (Axis 1)** | Belief elicitation; Bayesian updating tests | Harrison & Rutström 2008 |
| **Counterfactual interventions** | Causal inference (do-calculus framing) | Pearl 2009 |

The integrative move is combining these established methods into a single LLM-evaluation framework — not the methods themselves. This grounding is what gives the methodology paper its reviewer-defensibility: every load-bearing scoring decision has a 30-50-year-old citation behind it.

**Open**: the methodological-foundation table is an audit invitation. Reviewers spotting a load-bearing methodological choice without a cited anchor are exactly the engagement we want — those are the gaps the methodology paper needs to address before preprint circulation.

## The 3-layer pipeline

The three layers are the canonical revealed-preference pipeline applied to LLM behavior:

| Layer | Question | Method | What's measured |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 — existence | Does any rationalizing utility exist? | Axiomatic compliance: CCEI / GARP / WARP / transitivity / monotonicity / dominance | Pass/fail (per axiom) + degree of violation |
| 2 — identification | Within a chosen functional class, what utility parameters are consistent with observed choices? | MLE fit of KT prospect (λ, α) / CRRA / CARA / exp-vs-hyperbolic discount (δ, k) / Fehr-Schmidt (α, β) on training portion | Per-class point estimates + log-likelihood / AIC / BIC + Bayesian posterior over classes |
| 3 — predictive validity | Does the identified utility predict held-out choices? | Predict-then-validate: train/test split → identify on training → predict held-out → score predictive accuracy | Choice-agreement rate (discrete) + MAE (continuous) on held-out + oracle gap |

The methodology paper's load-bearing argument is that **integration** of all three layers is what enables actionable diagnostics. A single-number score at any one layer (the current state of the art) cannot tell you whether a model failed because its preferences are inconsistent, because its calibrated utility is wrong, or because it can't execute the choice the calibrated utility prescribes.

## The gate → grade reading: coherence is the floor, fidelity is the headline

The three layers read as a hierarchy, not three peers. Coherence (Layer 1) is a **precondition**: you cannot fit a utility (Layer 2) to choices that violate GARP, because no such utility exists. So the pipeline is a **gate → grade** structure:

- **Gate (Layer 1 — coherence/rationality)**: does *some* utility rationalize the choices? Pass/fail floor — coherence in the Andrews/GARP sense (Axis 2 below) *is* the gate. The 5-axis failure taxonomy is **wider than the gate**: it diagnoses failures both *at* the coherence gate (Axis 2) and *around* it — information (Axis 1), calibration (Axis 3), computational floor (Axis 4), meta-cognition (Axis 5) — spanning the gate and the downstream action, with persona-fidelity (the grade) as the headline.
- **Identification (Layer 2)**: fit the utility. Only well-defined once the gate passes.
- **Grade (Layer 3 — fidelity)**: does the fitted utility match the *configured or revealed* principal, and predict held-out behavior? The graded headline.

**Working position (engagement invited; see Q11).** Early toy runs suggest that for frontier models the gate is a **table-stakes floor** — they retrieve-and-compute the rational answer from problem *structure*, so contamination-style framings (non-textbook functional forms; recipe-traps where a memorized formula no longer applies) do not separate them. The discriminating signal is the **grade**: whether a model configured to a principal whose preferences deviate from the generically-rational answer *maintains* that configuration or *collapses* back to its default. This reframes the deployment verdict from "is the agent rational?" to "can the agent faithfully represent a configured principal under pressure?"

Two consequences for scoring:
- **The gate and grade must be scored separately**, because the failure sign is opposite: deviation from the generically-rational answer is a *failure* at the gate but can be a *success* at the grade (it is what a loss-averse principal wants). No single number can encode one behavior as both.
- **The grade surfaces a failure mode the gate cannot**: an agent can be perfectly coherent yet faithful to the *wrong* persona (coherent-but-unfaithful) — distinct from incoherence, and requiring a different fix.

**Why this is the alignment-relevant framing.** Persona-fidelity is **steerability**: can a deployed agent's revealed economic preferences be configured to its principal, or does it revert to training-default behavior under pressure? An agent that reverts is a faithful-delegation / principal-agent risk — the property the safety + economics-of-AI audience cares about, and what the grade layer measures directly.

**The grade has two orthogonal axes — steerability × framing-invariance.** *Steerability* = does the revealed utility shift to match a configured/revealed principal (intended drift — want high)? *Framing-invariance* = does it stay put under irrelevant surface reframing (unintended drift — want zero)? The counterfactual battery probes both (preference-flip = steerability; paraphrase-invariance = framing-invariance). Preliminary single-seed measurement: frontier defaults are barebone risk-neutral EV-maximizers and **framing-invariant** — across EV-identical surface reframings *and* classic gain/loss-relabel + social-nudge manipulations that move humans, the fitted implied utility did not budge — while steering cleanly under persona config. So the (model, prompt) dependence that matters is the *intentional* kind; "revealed utility" is well-defined holding the configuration fixed. (Caveat: grid resolution + single-seed; finer-grid + survival-framing + temperature>0 are the next-resolution probes.)

**Multi-seed update (N=20 bootstrap CIs).** The steerability-*magnitude* gap does NOT discriminate the two frontier models (overlapping CIs; both robustly steerable) — an earlier single-seed "collapse" was a gamble-grid artifact. The CI-survivable discriminators are *steering stability* (one model ~3× the variance) and *directional fidelity* (one persona direction disjoint). A *reinforced* persona also resisted an honest-persuasion adversarial attacker entirely (0% induced violation; the same pitches flip an unconfigured model). The persona-fit *mechanism* is separately validated on stylized investor personas (predict-then-validate diagonal dominance, beating an unconfigured default). The grade remains the discriminating layer, but via stability + directional fidelity, not collapse-magnitude — and multi-seed CIs are mandatory before any leaderboard claim. (This correction is itself a working-position demonstration: a preliminary single-seed result was downgraded by the rigorous version.)

**The EV-default is a liability (regime-blindness).** The framing-stable default is *risk-neutral EV-maximization*, which is optimal only in a narrow box (single-shot/additive, no ruin boundary, finite mean, risk-neutral principal, known probabilities, non-adversarial). Outside it a different utility is *objectively* better: **Kelly/log-utility** under compounding (time-average growth ≠ ensemble-average EV → EV-max over-bets to a.s. ruin even on +EV bets), **CVaR / drawdown-bounded** under an absorbing ruin barrier (ruin removes you from all future rounds, breaking EV's averaging assumption → bound the worst-tail), **CRRA(γ)** matched to a configured/revealed principal (a fiduciary optimizes the *principal's* utility, not the generic one), maxmin-EU under ambiguity. These are not four unrelated rules but **points on one CRRA-family × dynamics × barrier axis**: EV = γ=0 single-shot; Kelly = γ=1 *forced by* compounding; CVaR = a survival constraint layered on a hard barrier; CRRA-γ = the general configured-principal case. Selecting EV/Kelly/CVaR correctly given the environment's *dynamics* is **gate-side correctness**; matching the principal's *γ* is **grade-side fidelity**. So **steering off the EV-default toward the regime-appropriate utility is *correctness*, not just preference-matching** — a stronger leg for the grade. (Real deployment mappings — procurement/auction-at-scale → EV; reinvested-bankroll trading & cash-compounding agents → Kelly; insurance/reinsurance & regulatory-capital & runway-constrained agents → CVaR; client wealth-management & pension mandates & the 13F-investor persona case → CRRA-γ.) This makes framing-invariance double-edged: insensitivity to irrelevant *surface* reframing is good, but insensitivity to a *substantive* regime change (single-shot ↔ repeated-compounding, which should flip EV→Kelly) is **regime-blindness**. Probe (same favorable gamble, single-shot vs repeated-N-compounding, no Kelly hint), multi-seed across 18 gamble configs with Wilson/bootstrap CIs: one frontier model over-bets into negative-log-growth at the *first* compounding step (N=2: ~28% of configs, CI [12%, 51%]; ~17% severe instant-ruin) then converges to Kelly by N≥3 — i.e. regime-aware but adjusting one round late; the other applies Kelly uniformly, under-betting the single-shot (the safe direction). Both converge to per-seed Kelly at N≥3. The failure is real and CI-confirmed but localized to a one-round transition lag, not a flat ruinous policy; the single-seed *exact horizon* did not replicate (it shifted N=3→N=2) — the full bet-fraction-vs-horizon curve + CIs are load-bearing, not single-seed point values (the recurring lesson: summaries and single seeds mislead).

**Goal-dimension space (the generalization of the regime map).** The EV→Kelly→CVaR→CRRA axis above is the *WHAT* (risk-form) slice of a larger structure: an economic-agent goal is a **tuple, not a scalar**, and the "utility-max vs alignment" tension is a projection of that tuple onto one axis. Six dimensions, split into **objectives** and one **constraint**: WHAT (objective form — the regime map) · WHOSE (principal — generic↔configured, the grade) · WHEN (horizon — stress) · WITH WHOM (game structure — solo↔strategic↔cooperative, D2/multi-agent) · HOW WELL (epistemic calibration — Δ_inf) · **HOW (means legitimacy** — honest↔deceptive, the deception axis). The first five are objectives; **HOW is a constraint** carving the feasible strategy set. The resolution of the headline tension: **alignment lives in HOW (and partly WHOSE) — a constraint, not a competing objective** — so the "tradeoff" splits into (i) a *competence gap* (lost surplus because the best **allowed** strategy wasn't found — Δ_ctrl/regime-recognition; common, fixable) and (ii) a *genuine Pareto wall* (the highest-surplus strategy **was** the deceptive one; real but only in the binding band). [Hadfield-Menell et al. 2016](https://arxiv.org/abs/1606.03137) CIRL is the formal version (misalignment from *point-estimate* maximization, not maximization per se). The dimensions relate three ways — **lexicographic** (gate→grade; coherence and means-legitimacy are gated, not traded), **orthogonal** (calibration ⊥ horizon ⊥ risk-form — *why the score is a vector*; CS329H §5.4 separable preferences), and **genuine Pareto** (return↔risk, explore↔exploit, own↔joint surplus, short↔long horizon, fidelity↔generality, surplus↔means-legitimacy-in-the-binding-band). Three consequences: the score is a **goal-profile vector** (collapsing it = the fake-tradeoff error one level up — the recurring "report the profile, not the point"); **alignment-shift is a rotation in goal-space, per-dimension decomposable** (the generalized Q14 deception-axis readout); a **"frontier" claim is honest only on the genuine-Pareto pairs**. **Falsifier:** whether the five objective dimensions are truly orthogonal or secretly coupled is empirical (cf. the Layer-1+2-vs-residual claim, Q7) — the cross-axis correlation matrix tests it; strong coupling ⇒ the space is lower-dimensional than the taxonomy assumes.

**Why this is the durable bet (retrieval-resistance half-life).** A test resists textbook-retrieval only if there is no fixed answer to look up. Gate-side hardness — sequential inference, compositional computation, long-horizon coherence — is *temporarily* resistant and **decays** as models improve at multi-step execution (the fastest-moving capability). Grade-side hardness is *permanent*: "be faithful to *this* principal" has no textbook answer because the target is defined outside economics, by the principal — no capability gain makes it lookup-able. So the grade is the headline not only because it discriminates today but because its discriminating power **survives model improvement**. The Andon Labs Vending-Bench result is the live demonstration: a more capable, more aligned model (Opus 4.8) scores *worse* on the economic objective — capability rose, but the interesting variance moved to the grade-side property of *which objective governs behavior*.

**Stress is a cross-cutting axis (and the reason the gate stays in the design).** Rationality is a floor *at rest* but a cliff *under load*: the gate degrades under long horizon (Andon Vending-Bench — scam suppliers, non-convergent loops at 20M tokens), constrained compute budget, multi-agent strategic pressure, sequential-inference load, adversarial/meta-framing, and persona-conditioning (Wen 2025 — personas degrade GARP). So the 3-layer decomposition is kept intact; **stress is added as a dimension orthogonal to the layers**, and for each (model, task) the framework reports a *degradation curve* (gate-compliance + grade-fidelity vs stress level) and a *cliff edge*, not a pass/fail point. Stress-degradation does not decay with capability — the cliff edge moves further out but does not vanish (Opus 4.8 still fails long-horizon) — so it converts the otherwise-saturating gate into a moving-frontier measurement. One protocol consequence (Wen 2025): because persona-conditioning can itself break the gate, gate and grade are **co-measured under the same (persona, stress) condition**, not gate-once-at-baseline. ([Tak et al. 2026](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.22329) is the published anchor: reasoning-mode gains in rational-choice compliance come with heightened sensitivity to affective/emotional steering — evidence for stress-sensitive rationality, not generic adversarial reversibility.)

**External convergence (five economic-agent benchmarks, checked 2026-05-29).** [EconEvals](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.18825), [TERMS-Bench](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.13909), Andon Vending-Bench, [γ-Bench](https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.11807), and [Market-Bench](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.05523) independently draw the same line: single-agent / short-horizon / known-environment / given-parameters is **saturated** (γ-Bench 2-player Nash near-solved; TERMS deal-rate maxed; EconEvals steadily improving), while **multi-agent / long-horizon / competitive / adversarial / unknown-environment is where LLMs fail and discriminate** — strategic drift over long horizons (γ-Bench), winner-take-most capital appreciation (Market-Bench), scam-supplier susceptibility + non-convergence (Vending-Bench), surplus-extraction divergence (TERMS). That boundary is precisely the AERead stress + regime-appropriateness + alignment-shift axes — the field converges on "rationality-given-the-setup saturates; deployment-pressure regimes discriminate." Two implications: it de-risks the framing (we are not alone in finding the floor), and it opens AERead's natural v1+ application — **the trust/validation layer for agentic economic simulations** (all five are LLM-agent sims documenting agents failing under pressure; AERead is the diagnostic for *where* agents become untrustworthy as rational actors: which horizon, which regime, which alignment-version distorts the equilibrium). Complementary, not competitive; the deployment-readiness verdict extends cleanly.

**Adversarial stress-discovery** is the natural *generator* for the stress axis: rather than hand-enumerate stress dimensions, an attacker LLM searches for the scenarios that most degrade a defender (the system-under-test, optionally persona-configured), and the discovered exploits are frozen into the stress battery. Two constraints keep it valid and reproducible: the attacker's reward must be **mechanically/oracle-computed** (surplus extracted, GARP-violations induced, persona-drift, posterior distance) — never an LLM judge, per the judge-free commitment — so adversarial games must themselves be OracleDecomposable; and the adversarial phase is **test-generation, not runtime** (freeze discovered exploits → deterministic replay). This is the multi-agent / co-evolutionary direction (v0.5+/substrate scope; connects to the bargaining + agentic-marketplace candidates and the planned v2 adversarial-robustness dimension), and it is the strongest safety framing: automated red-teaming of whether a deployed economic agent can be made to betray its configured principal.

**Scope note (honest about v0).** v0's two shipped games (C2, C8) exercise the gate + identification + predictive-validity machinery; the persona-fidelity grade is demonstrated in a standalone persona-maintenance probe and matures into a named-principal instrument (C1 persona-fit on revealed preferences) in v0.5+. The gate→grade reading clarifies *where the discriminating signal is expected to live*, informing v0.5+ prioritization — it does not claim v0 already ships the full grade instrument.

**Falsification (pre-registered).** The "gate is a floor" position is falsified if, under the multi-class robustness battery, frontier models systematically separate on gate-layer axioms (one frontier model failing GARP / Bayes-optimal inference where another passes, beyond arithmetic noise). The "grade is the discriminator" position is falsified if frontier models do *not* separate on persona-fidelity under multi-seed evaluation with a per-persona fidelity metric that penalizes both under- and over-steering (so separation cannot be claimed from over-correction alone). Both are testable before the methodology paper circulates.

## Layer 3 design discipline: predict-then-validate

Every Layer 3 use case follows the same protocol:

1. **Train/test split** — partition observed decisions into N−K training + K held-out (fixed seed, immutable manifest).
2. **Layer 2 identification** — fit parameters within the assumed functional class using training only.
3. **Predict held-out** — deterministic prediction from fitted parameters + observable context.
4. **Predictive accuracy** — choice-agreement rate (for discrete choices) or MAE (for continuous emissions) on held-out decisions.

Multi-class robustness check: fit **3 candidate functional classes in v0** (KT prospect '79, CRRA, Kelly-Markowitz) on the training set; report a posterior over classes as a secondary score. CARA + cumulative prospect theory TK '92 are added in v0.5+. This is the response to [Andrews 2026](papers/pdfs/andrews_2026_revealed_rationality.pdf)'s non-identification critique: within-class identification is honest about which functional class assumption is doing the work, and the 3-class v0 already demonstrates the principle without requiring the full 5-class suite.

## OracleDecomposable: the load-bearing abstraction

A Layer 3 use case is `OracleDecomposable` if it admits:

- A **hidden state** (the principal's preferences, the vendor's true cost, etc.)
- A **controllable simulator** (we can substitute beliefs or reveal state)
- An **oracle policy** (the Bayes-optimal action given perfect knowledge of the hidden state)
- A **scalar utility function** over (action, hidden state)

Any such use case is **a candidate** for the [TERMS-Bench](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.13909) Eq. 4 decomposition: U(π★) − U(π_base) = Δ_inf + Δ_unc + Δ_ctrl (information gap + uncertainty gap + control gap). The "any such use case admits the decomposition" claim is **conditional** — TERMS-Bench's decomposition is simulator-relative and intervention-order-specific; the full conditions under which an OracleDecomposable task admits a well-defined Eq. 4 attribution (non-commutativity of interventions; Shapley/Sobol-style attribution when interventions don't commute; failure conditions when the abstraction breaks) are exactly what Zihao's Phase-0 (A) formalization scope ([proposal §9.2](proposal.md#92-zihao-li--proposed-phase-0-dol-econ--formal-math-foundation-track)) is meant to prove. Until that Phase-0 work lands, the OracleDecomposable interface is a **candidate abstraction with conditions to be formalized**, not a closed claim.

Zhang et al. 2026 introduced this decomposition for bilateral bargaining. We abstract the construction and demonstrate it on a *collection* of use cases. (Note on gate-saturation: TERMS-Bench is a gate-layer benchmark — its oracle is the generic-rational bargaining policy — and its [live leaderboard](https://terms-bench.github.io) shows the gate-as-floor pattern quantitatively: the agreement axis (AGR⁺) is saturated at 0.95–0.999 across the frontier-strong pack (the lone exception is the weak GPT-4o mini outlier at AGR⁺≈0.52) and procedural violations ≈ 0, with discrimination surviving only on surplus efficiency — and even there the frontier + strong-open pack is compressed to ~0.60–0.69 (the real gaps are to fixed baselines and one weak model). The coarse outcome saturates; the strategic/inference sub-decomposition is the more-durable-but-still-gate residual. Two corroborating signals: Claude Opus 4.6 outranks the newer Opus 4.7 on surplus extraction (a second independent instance, with Andon Vending-Bench, of the alignment↔economic-aggression tradeoff across model versions), and a multi-period survival view shows sub-frontier ruin while frontier + strong-open survive. The AERead-native value-add when we wrap bargaining (D2) is therefore the **persona-fidelity grade on top of the gate** — a *configured-principal* bargaining oracle, the axis that survives capability gains — not a gate-only wrap.) The v0 collection (2 deep games — scope discipline at v0 is what makes the first paper publishable rather than diffuse):

- **C2 ProductProcurementGame (v0)**: single-agent, one-shot, **discrete-action**; hidden state = vendor true cost + structured qualitative attributes (durability evidence / assembly friction / style fit etc.); **v0 oracle = optimal under single declared utility-weight vector per buyer profile** (per Q3 v0 scope — single-vector scoring); v0.5+ upgrade is the robust-utility-family extension (oracle = robust-optimal across plausible weight vectors). Deterministic preference-matching payoff. The qualitative-evidence procurement structure.
- **C8 SimplePricingGame (v0)**: single-agent, one-shot, **continuous-action** (price p ∈ [0, P_max]); hidden state = true demand-curve parameters (intercept α*, elasticity β*, noise σ*). **Designed for TERMS-Bench Eq. 4 decomposition** via four policies: (a) **base** = model sees declared prior + product description + **n=10 noisy historical (price, quantity) sales observations** → infers posterior + picks price; (b) **belief-substituted (posterior)** = model is given the Bayes-optimal posterior directly → picks price (isolates posterior-computation gap from price-choice gap); (c) **state-revealed** = true (α*, β*, σ*) revealed → must still compute the revenue-maximizing price itself (no closed-form hint); (d) **oracle (π★)** = the harness's closed-form revenue-maximum price α*/(2β*), kept separate from (c) so the residual is a genuine computational floor. Eq. 4 splits the gap as Δ_inf = U(post)−U(base) (inference) + Δ_unc = U(reveal)−U(post) (uncertainty) + Δ_ctrl = U(oracle)−U(reveal) (control), summing to U(π★)−U(base). **Structurally distinct from C2** on action space (continuous vs discrete) + outcome (stochastic revenue vs deterministic preference match) + oracle math (Bayesian-posterior argmax vs utility-vector dot product) + uncertainty representation (parametric demand curve + noisy evidence vs qualitative text). The pair (C2 + C8) demonstrates Eq. 4 across action-space variation — genuine structural distinction beyond uncertainty-type variation.

**Information-set relativity: why an "unverifiable domain" can still have an oracle.** TERMS-Bench targets negotiation — an *unverifiable* domain in the RLVR sense (no in-loop checker, hidden reservation values, you can't unit-test a bargaining move) — yet it ships an oracle. No contradiction: "verifiable" is indexed to an information set. The task is unverifiable *from the agent's seat*; the oracle is a *designer-side* construct (the author sets the hidden parameters, so can compute the full-information π★ offline). An oracle thus exists conditional on the designer's information while the task stays unverifiable conditional on the agent's. The cost is an **external-validity debt** — the oracle is a property of the *constructed* instance, not of real negotiation — which the process-level Δs (reasoning quality) carry across the synthetic→real gap better than absolute surplus does, and which predict-then-validate on *revealed* preference pays down by using reality, not an injected ground truth, as the oracle.

**Corollary: oracle-bearingness is manufacturable, so the question is regime, not verifiability.** It follows that a clean answer-verifier exists *not* iff the domain is intrinsically oracle-bearing, but iff the problem is oracle-bearing *from some accessible information set* — and a designer can almost always grant themselves that seat by planting the hidden state. Answer-verifiability is therefore **cheap to manufacture**; the load-bearing question is what *regime* the manufactured verifier lands in. TERMS is the worked case: its **deal-rate** sub-metric (just close *a* deal) saturates (AGR⁺ 0.95–0.999) while its **surplus** sub-metric (close the *Bayes-optimal* deal) discriminates — the *same* answer-verifier at two regime depths, `(primitive × regime)` visible inside one benchmark. But surplus-discrimination is **gate-under-stress with a half-life**: the stress is intrinsic strategic-inference depth, which decays as models improve (as deal-rate already did) — it is the "more-durable-but-still-gate residual," *not* the non-saturating grade. Reaching the grade requires re-pointing the oracle from the generic-rational π★ to a **configured-principal** π★ (verify against *this* principal's revealed preferences) — the fixed target disappears, and that is exactly the AERead-native value-add when wrapping bargaining (D2).

**"The environment is the verifier" = environment + reference; durability lives in the reference, not the environment.** A common framing (e.g. TERMS) is that in social-strategic domains *the environment itself acts as the verifier*. This conflates two separable things: (1) the environment **computes an outcome** (surplus, profit, deal/no-deal) — mechanical, judge-free, always available in a simulator; and (2) the outcome is interpretable as a **score**, which requires a **reference** to compare against (an oracle π★, a baseline, a competitor pool, or a principal). The environment alone gives only (1); pure environment-as-verifier with no reference is a competitive *arena* (Elo) — relative-only, no notion of "good," and it drifts with the opponent pool. So **the saturation behavior of an environment-as-verifier is determined entirely by which reference it scores against**, not by the environment: a generic-rational π★ reference saturates (gate-under-stress, half-life); a configured/revealed-principal reference is durable (no fixed target); a regime-forced property (Kelly under compounding) is durable (recognition, not lookup). The semi-verifiable zone — environment computes the outcome, designer supplies a real reference — is the right target *because* it is the only zone that is simultaneously rigorous (judge-free, unlike fully-unverifiable LLM-judge eval) and unsaturated (unlike clean-verifiable RLVR) — but only when the reference is the durable kind.

**What the Eq. 4 ladder actually measures (precision note).** The oracle is the omniscient ceiling, *not* the bar the agent's inference is held to — you can't fault an agent for not knowing hidden info. The fair bar for inference is the **posterior** (optimal Bayes on *accessible* signals). So **revealed vs posterior** = the gradeable error, itself split into **inference** (did it form the right belief from accessible signals?) and **control** (did it act optimally on whatever belief it held? — a distinct failure: an agent can update perfectly and still bargain badly); **oracle vs posterior** = **irreducible uncertainty**, factored *out* (not the model's fault). The decomposition's value is separating *unknowable* from *your fault*, and within *your fault*, *inference* from *control*.

## Evaluating no-oracle domains: how the grade scores without an oracle

If saturation tracks oracle-availability (see [`refocus.md`](refocus.md)), the discriminating problems are precisely the ones *without* a closed-form oracle — so "score against π★" is unavailable there. The grade substitutes other ground truths:

- **Reality, ex-post** — predict-then-validate on held-out outcomes; strictly-proper scoring (log/Brier/CRPS) makes truthful probabilistic reporting uniquely optimal without knowing the right action in advance.
- **Structure** — consistency/axiom checks (GARP/WARP, CCEI/Afriat), framing-invariance, steering-monotonicity: detect *violations of properties the optimum must have*, no external answer needed.
- **Local oracles** — decompose a no-oracle trajectory into sub-decisions that *do* have local optima (the OracleDecomposable move above, applied per-decision).
- **Reference / bounds** — regret vs a strong reference policy or a clairvoyant best-in-hindsight upper bound; dominance (catch the clearly-bad even when the best is unknown).
- **Revealed preference** — fit to a configured/observed principal you have data on (13F), validated out-of-sample.

The honest asymmetry: in the no-oracle regime you generally **cannot certify optimality**, but you *can* **falsify** it (axiom violation, dominated/ruinous choice, mispredicted held-out outcome, framing-sensitivity), **measure fit** (to revealed preference / realized outcome), and **score calibration** (proper scoring against resolved events). That is why the grade is graded on **fidelity + consistency + calibration**, never on optimality — this is the **econometric human-evaluation toolkit** (revealed-preference consistency, forecasting calibration, out-of-sample fit) applied to LLMs, appropriate precisely because LLMs have entered the no-oracle regime where humans always lived. **Caveat:** these primitives saturate in clean form and discriminate only under stress — saturation is a property of `(primitive × regime)`, not the primitive (see [`refocus.md`](refocus.md)).

## Generating cases at scale: the generator–verifier method

To mass-produce *novel* cases (contamination-resistant), exploit the **generation/verification asymmetry**: generating a hard instance is cheap and may be sloppy; trust lives entirely in the verifier. So the **generator can be an LLM** (propose scenarios, plant hidden states, write flavor text); the **verifier must be code** (judge-free), and the oracle/property must be **independently computed and cross-checked** (e.g. confirm closed-form π★ by brute-force search; a generator that also computes its own labels silently corrupts every case). Which axis a generator reaches depends on its verifier channel:

| Verifier channel | Checks | Lands on | Saturation |
|---|---|---|---|
| parametric / designer-side oracle | the **answer** vs π★ | gate | saturated at rest |
| same oracle, **stressed** (long horizon / tight budget / distractor / strategic depth) | the **answer**, difficulty in the regime | gate-under-stress | discriminates (with a half-life) |
| **property / consistency** | a **law the optimum must obey** — *no answer needed* (GARP; framing-invariance; the regime-forced bet-fraction ordering) | gate **and** grade | discriminates |
| **predict-then-validate** | **reality, ex-post** (13F / market) | grade (fidelity) | durable, noisy oracle |

The move that makes mass-production of *discriminating* cases possible is **verify the relationship, not the answer**: procedurally vary one knob (e.g. single-shot → compounding-N → ruin-barrier) and check the *ordering the regime forces* — you never compute "the one right bet," you verify a monotone law any correct agent must respect. Mechanically checkable, not textbook-lookupable, and lands in the stressed/no-oracle regime. Four pitfalls: **novelty ≠ difficulty** (fresh numbers in a saturated template are still saturated — procedural generation buys contamination-resistance for free, but discrimination only if the case lands in a stressed/no-oracle regime); **verifier-gaming** (a scalar verifier invites the degenerate policy — pair property-checks with a fit signal); **off-manifold realism** (an unconstrained generator makes verifiable-but-weird cases that don't transfer — constrain to a plausible distribution or anchor to real data); **oracle-bug = systematically wrong labels** (independent computation + cross-check is non-negotiable; a wrong verifier certifies the wrong thing while looking rigorous). This is the engine for the four-regime battery: the regime-blindness probe is a hand-built prototype of exactly this pattern.

**Why bargaining-Bayes is hard (mechanism behind nonzero Δ_inf).** Three reasons frontier models leak surplus on the inference step even though clean conjugate Bayes is saturated: (1) **the likelihood is a strategic-equilibrium object** — to read what an offer "means" you must invert the adversary's strategy (signaling-game / PBE inference, often partially pooling), not condition on i.i.d. data, and the exact posterior has no closed form; (2) **the post-training cooperative default is anti-Bayesian** — it under-discounts self-serving cheap talk and anchors on stated numbers, biasing the posterior toward the counterparty's claim (the RLHF-coverage thread); (3) **no externalized sequential belief state** — the belief is implicit and re-derived each turn, producing order effects (non-commutativity) and compounding drift over rounds. This is why **surplus discriminates while deal-rate saturates**. Pre-registered falsifier: if an explicit belief-tracking scaffold (externalized posterior + per-round update) closes most of Δ_inf, the deficit is mechanism (3) not (1) — a clean cheap experiment for the D2 wrap.

## The 5-axis failure taxonomy

When a model scores low on a Layer 3 task, *which mechanism failed?* The 5-axis taxonomy decomposes the gap. The five axes are the **anatomy of failure at and around the gate** — coherence (Axis 2) is the gate proper, while information (1), calibration (3), computational floor (4), and meta-cognition (5) diagnose failures around the gate and in the downstream action. Persona-fidelity (the grade) is a distinct *type* of measurement (graded, opposite failure-sign, noisy oracle), **not a sixth axis** of the same kind (see the gate→grade section + Q11).

**v0 instrumentation tiers** (5-axis taxonomy stays intact as the paper's §3 claim; instrumentation depth varies):

| Axis | v0 instrumentation | v0.5+ extension |
|---|---|---|
| **Axis 1 — Information** | Full Δ_inf measurement per OracleDecomposable (TERMS-Bench Eq. 4) | Belief-substitution beyond state-reveal |
| **Axis 2 — Consistency** | 2 axiom tests (transitivity + dominance) per game | Full 6-axiom family (CCEI/GARP/WARP/transitivity/monotonicity/Houtman-Maks) |
| **Axis 3 — Calibration** | Brier score + within-class utility-fit error per Q1 multi-class | ECE diagnostic + reliability diagrams + full proper-scoring-rule suite |
| **Axis 4 — Computational floor** | 1 quantity-substitution probe per game (sketched only) | Full computation-substitution suite (LP-substitution / DP-substitution / Bayes-update-substitution) |
| **Axis 5 — Meta-cognitive** | Confidence elicitation + Brier calibration on held-out (sketched only) | Full Dutch-book / probabilistic-identity / money-pump probe suite |

**Why this split**: the 5-axis taxonomy paper claim is the integrative framework; v0 demonstrates it by **fully instrumenting axes 1-3** + **sketching axes 4-5 with 1 sub-test each**. Reviewers see the full taxonomy in the paper; the implementation is partial-but-honest. v0.5+ extensions are pre-committed in the methodology paper's "Future work" section + the v0.5+ extension column above.

| # | Axis | Causal hypothesis | Prior-art anchor |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Information** | Agent lacks knowledge of hidden state | TERMS-Bench (Zhang et al. 2026) — generalized via OracleDecomposable |
| 2 | **Consistency** | Agent's preference / belief structure violates axioms | Andrews 2026 (representation theorem penalties); [Chen et al. 2023](https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.2316205120) (at-scale CCEI — frontier LLMs near-saturate GARP, avg CCEI ≈ 0.998 > humans) |
| 3 | **Calibration** | Agent's economic parameters are misfit (Layer 2 identification fails) | [Mazeika 2025](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.08640) (utility engineering); [Guo et al. 2017](https://arxiv.org/abs/1706.04599) (ECE diagnostic); [Gneiting & Raftery 2007](papers/pdfs/gneiting_raftery_2007_strictly_proper_scoring_rules.pdf) (proper scoring rules) |
| 4 | **Computational floor** | Agent can't execute the math the choice requires | Specific quantity-substitution operationalization; EconEvals 2025 competency-as-prerequisite is the closest precedent (we extend it from pre-check to per-axis decomposition) |
| 5 | **Meta-cognitive** | Agent's confidence is uncalibrated (stated ≠ realized accuracy) | [Yamin et al. 2026](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.06286) (belief coherence); [Chadwick 2025](papers/pdfs/chadwick_kahng_kipper_2025_dutch_books.pdf) (Dutch books); [Zhu & Griffiths 2024](https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.16646) (incoherent probability judgments) |

**Axis 5 footnote — calibration vs. coherence as complementary, not redundant.** Following Andrews 2026's framing, we treat *calibration* ([Guo et al. 2017](https://arxiv.org/abs/1706.04599); [Gneiting & Raftery 2007](papers/pdfs/gneiting_raftery_2007_strictly_proper_scoring_rules.pdf)'s proper scoring rules) and *coherence* (de Finetti / Dutch-book diagnostics) as **complementary** properties. Calibration asks whether predicted probabilities match empirical frequencies — it requires ground-truth outcomes and operates event-by-event. Coherence asks whether stated probabilities are internally consistent — it requires no ground truth and operates across multiple related assessments. A model can be well-calibrated on many events yet incoherent across them, or coherent yet poorly calibrated. AERead's Axis 5 reports both signals separately: calibration error against the held-out validation set (Layer 3 outcomes), and coherence violations under Dutch-book / proper-scoring-rule probes (no ground truth needed).

**Methodological gotcha — strict propriety for the leaderboard score.** Gneiting & Raftery 2007 establishes that a leaderboard score (which models are incentivized to maximize) must use a **strictly proper** scoring rule, otherwise it can be gamed by deliberate miscalibration. Expected Calibration Error (Guo et al. 2017) is the field-standard *diagnostic* metric but is **not strictly proper** — it can be lowered by shifting probability mass away from extreme bins without improving the underlying forecast. AERead therefore uses **Brier score (discrete events) and Continuous Ranked Probability Score / CRPS (continuous emissions)** for the leaderboard headline, both of which are strictly proper per Gneiting & Raftery's §3-§4. ECE + reliability diagrams remain on the diagnostic page for interpretability, but they do not enter the ranking.

Each axis has a distinct intervention mechanism:
- **Axis 1**: substitute beliefs / reveal state and rerun
- **Axis 2**: present counterfactual choice triplets and check axiom violations
- **Axis 3**: hold out test decisions, refit, compare predictive accuracy
- **Axis 4**: substitute the computational quantity with a closed-form answer
- **Axis 5**: elicit confidence + compare to realized accuracy for calibration (Brier score on held-out events; ECE + reliability diagrams for diagnostic visualization); run Dutch-book + money-pump constructions (Chadwick 2025) and probabilistic-identity probes (Zhu & Griffiths 2024) for coherence (no ground truth needed)

Cross-decomposition (Axis 1 × Axis 2, etc.) produces an attribution matrix with main-effect cells + pairwise interaction cells + residual. **Whether main effects explain a high fraction of the per-(model, task) gap is a per-domain empirical hypothesis** (per Q7 working ranges: 60-75% discrete-action procurement (v0 C2), 50-70% continuous-action pricing (v0 C8), declining to 30-55% multi-agent markets and 25-50% long-horizon agents — see [`methodology_open_questions.md` § Q7](methodology_open_questions.md#q7--how-much-of-the-per-model-task-gap-do-layers-1--2-explain-whats-the-residual) for the full table). This is pre-registered with falsification criteria per domain, not assumed as a uniform threshold. Interactions between axes are expected (e.g., calibration depends on whether the agent has the relevant information; computational-floor failures can masquerade as inconsistent preference) — the factorial design measures these rather than assuming them away.

**Orthogonality, not residual — what Axes 2/5 + the grade add beyond TERMS Eq.4.** A reviewer will note that the TERMS-Bench Δ_inf + Δ_unc + Δ_ctrl decomposition is an *exact telescoping identity* within a single OracleDecomposable task — the three factors always sum to U(π★)−U(base), with **zero residual by construction**. So the extra factors do *not* mop up Eq.4 residual (there is none); they measure objects the per-task gap-to-oracle structurally cannot see, and are therefore orthogonal **scope extensions**, not residual-capture. Two distinct "residual" notions must not be conflated: the *Eq.4 telescoping residual* (within-task) = 0; the *attribution-matrix residual* (Q7, above) is the empirical cross-axis quantity. What the additions capture: **Axis 2 (Consistency)** = cross-choice coherence (GARP / Afriat CCEI), not a per-task gap — a model can have a small Eq.4 gap on every task yet be incoherent across them. (Consistency is robustly *near-saturated* for frontier LLMs — Chen et al. 2023 (PNAS) find even GPT-3.5 reaches avg CCEI ≈ 0.998 across 100 budget choices, exceeding humans — so Axis 2 functions as a near-saturated gate, a different object from the per-task Eq.4 gap.) **Axis 5 (Meta-cognitive)** = belief-about-performance vs performance (calibration ⊥ accuracy), not the gap magnitude; **the grade (persona-fidelity)** = fidelity to a *persona-relative* oracle, which changes what π★ is — a different decomposition entirely. Outside OracleDecomposable tasks Eq.4 does not apply at all, and these factors carry the whole load. (A full empirical orthogonality demonstration — a cross-model correlation matrix over all axes — requires the multi-model eval; preliminary toy runs give existence proofs: consistent-but-unfaithful, accurate-but-miscalibrated, computes-the-optimum-but-collapses-on-persona.)

Concretely, beyond the three Eq.4 factors the framework surfaces (grade-side unless noted): **steerability** (revealed-utility shift to a configured principal), **persona-collapse / reinforcement-decay** (does the config hold without per-decision restatement), **framing-invariance** (utility drift under EV-identical reframing — the confound-control for steerability), **self-revealed-utility predictive validity** (Layer-2 fit + held-out Layer-3 — which utility the model reveals, not the scalar gap), **which-objective-governs** (configured vs rational vs safety-trained default), plus gate-side **cross-choice CCEI** and **meta-cognitive calibration**, and a **negative information-value** phenomenon (meta-guidance such as an axiom prompt or a population prior *degrading* behavior — outside Eq.4's monotone hidden-state information ladder). Signal (now with multi-seed CIs on the grade metrics): the grade-side metrics discriminate via *steering stability + directional fidelity* (the steerability-*magnitude* gap itself has overlapping CIs across frontier models) + the negative-information-value phenomenon; the Eq.4 factors + consistency + calibration come up near-floor for frontier models — consistent with rationality-as-gate. Stress-degradation is separately confirmed (compute-budget cliff located per model; persona-conditioning degrades the weaker model's consistency — the gate×grade interaction).

### Relation to the diagnostic-and-correction literature

A growing body of work documents LLM rationality violations: [Betz & Richardson 2023](https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0281372) (probabilistic coherence as an epistemic-agent property; with a training-step correction), [Chen et al. 2023](https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.12763) (emergence of economic rationality), [Hagendorff et al. 2023](https://arxiv.org/abs/2212.05206) (thinking-fast-and-slow patterns), Zhu & Griffiths 2024 (incoherent probability judgments), Chadwick et al. 2025 (Dutch-book / money-pump diagnostics + post-processing correction), [Wen 2025](https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.18190) (persona-degrades-GARP), and [Qiu et al. 2026](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.17523) (Bayesian-teaching training corrections). AERead's contribution relative to this literature is the **integrative** stance: rather than documenting one violation type at a time, the 5-axis taxonomy organizes all of them into a single attribution framework anchored on Andrews 2026's representation-theorem "if and only if" guarantees — if the per-axis penalty terms can be driven to zero, the representation theorems imply that there must exist a prior + utility + computational policy + calibration map that fully explain the model's behavior. The diagnostic page is what makes that attribution legible.

Betz & Richardson 2023's §Future Research explicitly calls for "improved diagnostic tools" with "high-resolution representations of an agent's probabilistic belief system" and the ability to relate the inferential structure of training data to current model behavior. **AERead's 5-axis cross-decomposition + drill-down diagnostic page is exactly the artifact they called for.**

## Five methodological commitments

Each citation-anchored:

1. **Descriptive measurement, not normative ranking.** Higher composite scores ≠ better-aligned. *Andrews 2026 §7.*
2. **Measurement-model pluralism.** 4-6 competing models per axiom; report Bayesian posterior. *Echenique 2021 / Davis-Stober.*
3. **Judge-free by construction.** No LLM evaluates another LLM. All three anchor papers (TERMS-Bench May 2026; Revealed Rationality Feb 2026; EconEvals March 2025) independently adopted this. *TERMS-Bench / EconEvals / QEDBench.*
4. **Context-conditional axiomatization.** Personas degrade axiom compliance; this is a measurable dimension, not an assumption to control. *Wen 2025.*
5. **Cross-layer attribution + oracle-gap decomposition.** What turns scores into actionable signals.

## Adoption commitment: plug-and-play usability

Plug-and-play is an **adoption** commitment, not a methodology commitment — separate from the five above. Adoption traction analysis (25 benchmarks studied) found that no benchmark > 200 cites lacks (a) a live submission leaderboard with auto-scoring, (b) a frontier-lab model-card commitment, and (c) ≤ 5-minute clone-to-first-output. Therefore AERead is designed for:

- Zero-code task contribution via YAML
- One-line evaluation: `pip install aeread && aeread evaluate --model claude-opus-4-7 --task all`
- **v0**: one submission path (web form); Colab + PR paths defer to v0.5+
- Integration AS lm-evaluation-harness task family — so labs already running `lm_eval` have us installed automatically
- Cache layer never invalidates — good-hygiene reproducibility (cache hashes externally auditable; signed-ScoreRecord cryptographic provenance defers to v0.5+)

## What this is not

- It is not a normative test of "rationality." Higher AERead scores ≠ better-aligned models.
- It is not a substitute for safety / hallucination / RAG benchmarks. It is the rationality dimension that those benchmarks do not measure.
- It is not a single-number leaderboard. It is a fingerprint along multiple axes, and the diagnostic page is the load-bearing artifact.
- It does not claim to invent the decomposition methods. Each pillar is an extension of an existing piece of literature; the integration is the novel contribution.
